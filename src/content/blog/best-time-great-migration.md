Everyone wants the crossing. The columns of wildebeest plunging into
crocodile-thick water is the single most requested sight in East Africa — and
also the most misunderstood.

## The window is narrower than you think

The herds are in the Maasai Mara roughly **July through October**, but the
dramatic Mara River crossings are concentrated in space (a handful of
traditional crossing points) and triggered by weather and grazing pressure, not
a calendar. Peak crossing activity is typically **mid-August to late
September** — but the herds can arrive earlier or linger later.

## How to improve your odds

- **Give it days, not hours.** A single afternoon at the river is a lottery
  ticket. Three or four full days working the crossing points turns the odds in
  your favour.
- **Stay close to the Mara Triangle.** Most crossings happen on the western
  side of the reserve. A camp inside or adjacent to the Triangle saves precious
  morning hours.
- **Trust the guide, not GPS.** Our Mara-specialists read wildebeest behaviour —
  the massing, the false starts — and move on credible radio reports.
