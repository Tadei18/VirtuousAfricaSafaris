Most first-time travellers over-pack. Bush camps do same-day laundry, luggage on
light aircraft is weight-limited, and you'll live in about four outfits.

## The short list

- **Neutral, breathable layers.** Khaki, olive, brown. Mornings are cold, middays
  are hot — you'll add and shed layers constantly.
- **A warm fleece or puffy.** Pre-dawn game drives in an open vehicle are
  genuinely cold, even on the equator.
- **A wide-brim hat and real sunglasses.** The equatorial sun is not subtle.
- **Binoculars.** One decent pair per person changes the whole trip. 8x42 is the
  sweet spot.
- **A soft-sided bag.** Light aircraft require it; hard cases often don't fit.

## Leave the rest

Skip the bright colours (they spook wildlife and, in tsetse country, attract
bites), the excess of "just in case" clothes, and the heavy zoom you've never
used. We'll send a tailored kit list once your itinerary is set.
